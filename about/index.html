Scratch Animator

I got interested in animation techniques, effective art styles, production and supportive technology. The productions of Studio Ghibli have many details integrated, but are enormosly intensive to make. With ponyo they used a simplfied art style to give more detail to the animations instead of the image, but the reception was mixed. How can you produce a pure animative experience without comprimising on visual image needs, while also being condensely packed? Looom got an interesting approach where they only support weave animations. Their app seemed approachble from a usability standpoint and people where happy with it, so I thought to create my own specific tool and workflow with the new found knowledge of User interface Nodes in Godot. Loosly taken inspirations from Behind the Design: Looom and Loom user guide. The , which also includes the latest scratch_canvas addons.

A creative program with a minimal user interface. üì•Get the Godot project source files as zip or View it on Github.

Inception

Before Mondriaan Maker there was resizable rectangles. It was a fixed size of 3 by 3 rectangles each expanding, but their edges resizable. The next step was adding colors to the rectangles. By accident I made something that reminded me of an Mondriaan painting, and so I applied an mondriaan palette. Then came the infinite rectangles update, scaling up the possibilities of programm. It took some time to polish the interaction, but the coloring bar could be replaced by mutating colors and color dragging. The screenshot button could be replaced by an timed screenshot popup.
